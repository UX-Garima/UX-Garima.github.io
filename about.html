About me

My journey began with a background in Life Sciences and Education, where I initially focused on understanding child development and educational theories—centered around meeting the needs of young learners. As my career evolved, I transitioned into UX design, shifting my focus to understanding and meeting the diverse needs of users across digital platforms.

This transition from child-centered theories to user-centered design has been transformative. It's about moving beyond theoretical frameworks aimed at specific demographics to creating digital experiences that resonate with and cater to the real-world needs and behaviors of all users. In UX design, I blend my knowledge of human behavior with creative problem-solving to craft interfaces that are not only functional but also intuitive and enjoyable to use.

Self-Learning Adventures

Driven by curiosity, I embarked on a path of continuous learning. I believe in the power of ongoing education and improvement—it's a never-ending loop. From completing Google’s UX Design Professional Certificate to diving into hands-on projects, each experience has enriched my skills and broadened my perspective in UX design. I'm always eager to explore new techniques and innovations to enhance user experiences.
